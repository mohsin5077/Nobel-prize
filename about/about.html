Why the Nobel Prizes Were Created?

After reading his negative obituary, Nobel decided to leave a legacy that would be remembered positively. The Nobel Prizes were meant to honor those who contributed significantly to society, acknowledging excellence in Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, and Peace. He wanted to inspire future generations to work towards progress, knowledge, and peace. Nobel’s will also stated that the prize money should be substantial, ensuring that the awards would be a lasting and influential recognition of excellence. He chose the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences, the Karolinska Institute, and the Norwegian Nobel Committee to oversee the prizes in the respective fields. The Nobel Peace Prize, in particular, was intended to honor those who had made significant efforts towards peace and resolving conflicts—an area where Nobel felt the greatest personal connection due to the destructive potential of his inventions.
